Chapter 1

Cavallini Provider Acceptance, Implant System Verification, Sedation Fit, and Warranty Boundary Review

Why this topic matters

Use this chapter from first clinic contact through the end of the initial 10-business-day due diligence cycle and complete it before domain-02 records assembly, domain-03 cost modeling, or domain-04 travel booking begins.

Failure patterns
  • Risk: Implant brand ambiguity survives into commitment. Mitigation: require written implant system confirmation and expected discharge component records before any deposit or airfare purchase.
  • Risk: Sedation is advertised but later denied or delayed after medical review. Mitigation: document all sedation prerequisites by day minus 21 and keep travel tentative until suitability is bounded.
  • Risk: The treatment proposal hides a staged or multi-trip workflow. Mitigation: force explicit labeling of same-trip versus staged sequence before domain-03 cost modeling begins.
  • Risk: Lifetime warranty is assumed to function like local aftercare. Mitigation: classify warranty utility only after extracting exclusions, maintenance obligations, and patient-borne travel implications.
  • Risk: Onsite hotel, lab, and pharmacy are treated as guaranteed controls without checking availability or limits. Mitigation: log actual case relevance, timing constraints, and support boundaries in the due diligence file.
  • Risk: Fast responses are mistaken for reliable planning. Mitigation: score communication quality on consistency and document delivery, not speed alone.
  • Risk: Contradictions are solved informally by phone and never documented. Mitigation: convert every contested item into one of four statuses only: confirmed, confirmed with bounded exception, rejected, or REQUIRES CLARIFICATION.
